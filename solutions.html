Protecting Your Cookies Against Malice

Wait, what? How does it get worse than ads scraping your cookies for targeting information?

Glad you asked.

Remember that cookies are just text stored in your browser, and that websites read those cookies to make decisions.

For example, an autofill cookie contains a unique ID stored on your computer that matches with a hash on the website that will autofill your login information in the appropriate fields.

If a criminal were to intercept or read that cookie, they could set a cookie with the same name and value as your cookie, and gain access to your account without having to guess your username or password.

Because your cookies contain information about almost all your internet activity, a breach to your cookie security can have far-reaching consequences.

Cookies, because of their simplicity, can represent the weakest link in your personal privacy.

So, what can you do about it? Is disabling all cookies an option?

It is certainly possible to block all cookies from your device with cybersecurity applications or browser extensions, but this nuclear option is not recommended.

Remember that websites need some cookies to perform basic functions, and without them, some sites may not work.
